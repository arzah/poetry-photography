Oceanic Dreams







"There’s nothing more beautiful
than the way the ocean refuses to stop kissing the shoreline,
no matter how many times it’s sent away." -Sarah Kay





There is nothing more true to humans then the sea
Our emotions intertwine
Rough and stormy is our anger
Calm and serene is our peace
Cloudy and dark is our uncertainty
Indeed
There is nothing more true to humans then the sea

To live underwater
Is to live in peace
Away from human interference
And impurities of land
Ignorant to the world above

A frozen nirvana.
As each flake falls
A white blanket covers the Earth
Creating a silence,
A stillness,
A cold world,
That will eventually melt
And give birth to new life.





Earthly Beings







“Hills of forest green where the mountains touch the sky,
a dream come true,
I’ll live there til I die.” — Don McLean





She is young,
Her surroundings dark
And claustrophobic,
There is no space.

And then she breaks free
Turning her pettled head to the sun
She faces the light
And embraces it

Perhaps everyone needs to get lost
In order to be found
As it is when you’re lost
That you discover
All of the beautiful paths in life

The hotel is lone
Abandoned
The birds veer away
And the clouds part
Only mother nature remains
Reclaiming her lost child




Heavenly Array




“We shall find peace.
We shall hear angels,
we shall see the sky sparkling with diamonds.” – Anton Chekhov

We mimic Icarus
Flying close to the sun
The freedom of it all
The breeze caressing our skin
And the glorious Earth beneath us.

But like Icarus
We fall.
Though instead of despair,
We are happy.
As after the fall
We can climb again.

The fog is silent as it slithers in,
A serpent winding its way through the streets
engulfing everything in its path.
It’s hunger is insatiable.

But it soon stops,
Watching over the city.
And then moves on
As quietly as it came

When the sky is set ablaze
And the sun has gone to rest
Search for me in the horizon
